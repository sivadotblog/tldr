### Attacks & Vulnerabilities

[### Interlock Ransomware Group Leaks 43GB of Data in City of St. Paul Cyberattack (3 minute read)](https://hackread.com/interlock-ransomware-leaks-st-paul-city-cyberattack-data/?utm_source=tldrinfosec)

The Interlock ransomware group attacked the City of St. Paul, disrupting payments and essential services. The city refused to pay the ransom and launched Operation Secure St. Paul with support from the FBI and National Guard while investigating the breach. Interlock claims to have leaked 43GB of HR, financial, and identification documents, prompting a citywide password reset and warnings about increased phishing risks.

[### New Downgrade Attack Can Bypass FIDO Auth In Microsoft Entra ID (2 minute read)](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-downgrade-attack-can-bypass-fido-auth-in-microsoft-entra-id/?utm_source=tldrinfosec)

ProofPoint researchers discovered a new downgrade attack on Entra ID that downgrades the MFA from FIDO to a less secure fallback. The attack uses a custom evilgnix attacker-in-the-middle module to spoof a browser agent that lacks FIDO support, such as Safari for Windows. It can be prevented by disabling fallback methods.

[### Hackers leak 2.8M sensitive records from Allianz Life in Salesforce data breach (2 minute read)](https://securityaffairs.com/181093/data-breach/hackers-leak-2-8m-sensitive-records-from-allianz-life-in-salesforce-data-breach.html?utm_source=tldrinfosec)

Threat actors linked to ShinyHunters have leaked 2.8 million records from Allianz Life's Salesforce CRM, exposing personal details including names, addresses, birth dates, and Tax IDs of customers and business partners. The July 16 breach occurred through social engineering attacks targeting the third-party cloud CRM system, affecting the majority of Allianz Life's 1.4 million customers. The attackers have formed a joint Telegram channel with other groups, including Scattered Spider and Lapsus$, to claim credit for this and other major Salesforce-related breaches.

[### Detection Engineering: Practicing Detection-As-Code (6 minute read)](https://blog.nviso.eu/2025/07/08/detection-engineering-practicing-detection-as-code-introduction-part-1/?utm_source=tldrinfosec)

This is part one of an ongoing series on implementing detection-as-code (DaC). [### What the LockBit 4.0 Leak Reveals About RaaS Groups (5 minute read)](https://www.darkreading.com/vulnerabilities-threats/what-lockbit-leak-reveals-raas-groups?utm_source=tldrinfosec)

LockBit 4.0's leaked affiliate panel exposes a fragmented, chaotic RaaS operation: inconsistent negotiations, broken decryptors, unpaid platform fees, and even hits on prohibited Russian targets. This unpredictability complicates defense and incident response. The takeaway: don't rely on negotiation‚Äîprioritize preparation (segmentation, MFA, patching, and lateral-movement monitoring), as RaaS brands splinter and attribution grows harder.
