### Attacks & Vulnerabilities

[### Everest Ransomware Claims Mailchimp as New Victim in Relatively Small Breach (2 minute read)](https://hackread.com/everest-ransomware-claims-mailchimp-small-breach/?utm_source=tldrinfosec)

Everest ransomware group claims to have breached Mailchimp, stealing 767 MB of data containing 943,536 lines, including business information, domain names, company emails, and technology stack details. The leaked data appears to be structured business information from marketing or CRM exports rather than Mailchimp's internal systems, suggesting the breach may have targeted customer data rather than core infrastructure. Everest follows the double extortion model and previously breached Coca-Cola in May, though it remains a relatively obscure ransomware strain compared to major groups.

[### Apple patches security flaw exploited in Chrome zero-day attacks (2 minute read)](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/apple-patches-security-flaw-exploited-in-chrome-zero-day-attacks/?utm_source=tldrinfosec)

Apple patched CVE-2025-6558, a high-severity vulnerability in the ANGLE graphics layer that was actively exploited in Chrome zero-day attacks and allows remote code execution via malicious HTML pages. Google's Threat Analysis Group discovered the flaw in June, with Google patching Chrome on July 15 and CISA adding it to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog, requiring federal agencies to patch by August 12. The vulnerability affects iOS 18.6, iPadOS 18.6, macOS Sequoia 15.6, and other Apple platforms, marking the sixth zero-day Apple has patched this year.

[### Balancing Secrecy and Transparency in Bug Bounty Programs (6 minute read)](https://cacm.acm.org/opinion/balancing-secrecy-and-transparency-in-bug-bounty-programs/?utm_source=tldrinfosec)

Bug bounty programs allow vendors to expand their security testing efforts with community hackers who can receive payment for their work. One issue with this approach is that, particularly in private programs, vendors control the disclosure timeline and may create an information gap. Government organizations should step in to regulate vulnerability disclosure with NIST drafting standards and metrics for bug bounty programs, CISA establishing a voluntary vendor accreditation program, as well as requiring federal agencies to comply with the standards, and the SEC to require publicly traded companies to report their bug bounty metrics.

[### Hackers plant 4G Raspberry Pi on bank network in failed ATM heist (2 minute read)](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/hackers-plant-4g-raspberry-pi-on-bank-network-in-failed-atm-heist/?utm_source=tldrinfosec)

The UNC2891 hacking group (LightBasin) physically planted a 4G-equipped Raspberry Pi on a bank's ATM network switch to create covert remote access into the internal network. The attackers used this device as a pivot point to move laterally through the bank's systems, deploying backdoors and attempting to install their Caketap rootkit to spoof ATM authorization and steal cash. The heist ultimately failed, but demonstrated advanced anti-forensics techniques, including filesystem mounting to hide malicious processes from security tools.

A common metric of security program success is how much leadership is prepared to stick with the work over the long term. Security work tends to follow a cycle in which security issues are increasing so the company hires a security engineer to fix them, the engineer begins fixing the low hanging fruit and the number of issues declines, the engineer digs deeper and implements more logging, monitoring, etc and discovers more issues, management wonders why the issues have increased and fires the engineer.
