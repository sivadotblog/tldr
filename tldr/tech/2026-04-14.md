[### OpenAI touts Amazon alliance in memo, says Microsoft has ‘limited our ability' to reach clients (4 minute read)](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/13/openai-touts-amazon-alliance-in-memo-microsoft-limited-our-ability.html?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

OpenAI's revenue chief, Denise Dresser, recently sent a memo to staff saying that the company's alliance with Amazon was a key growth driver for its enterprise business. The memo said that OpenAI's partnership with Microsoft was foundational to its success, but it limited the startup's ability to meet enterprises' needs. OpenAI's enterprise business makes up 40% of its revenue and is expected to meet parity with its consumer business by the end of the year. The company has been trying to convince investors of its strengthening position as it is gearing up for an initial public offering as soon as this year.

[### Amazon Leo Shows Off In-Flight Wi-Fi Antenna That Will Take on Starlink (3 minute read)](https://www.pcmag.com/news/amazon-leo-shows-off-in-flight-wi-fi-antenna-that-will-take-on-starlink?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Amazon's Leo Aviation Antenna is a satellite dish designed specifically for in-flight Wi-Fi in planes. The flat, low-profile antenna is designed to be installed on the exterior of the plane to receive radio signals from orbiting low-Earth satellites. Amazon promises a single-day installation for its antenna - SpaceX's terminals require a downtime of 10 to 14 days. Amazon's airline satellite service promises to deliver up to 1 Gbps in downloads and 400Mbps in uploads simultaneously.
