### Big Tech & Startups

[### Tesla's dedicated Optimus factory construction officially underway at Giga Texas (14 minute read)](https://www.teslarati.com/tesla-dedicated-optimus-factory-construction-officially-underway-giga-texas/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Tesla's new Optimus factory is officially under construction at Gigafactory Texas. The project will add more than 5.2 million square feet of new industrial space. Tesla plans to build about 27,000 Optimus robots a day at the new factory. Production will start either in July or August of this year, with high-volume output starting in Summer 2027.

[### Apple Developing iPhone Anti-Snatching Feature That Locks Stolen Phones Instantly (2 minute read)](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/05/27/apple-anti-theft-iphone-feature/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Apple is working on a feature that will lock users' phones if the system detects that the device has been snatched from their hands. The feature will use the gyroscope, accelerometer, and other sensors to determine when a device has been grabbed. It is unknown when the feature may be added to iOS. Android already has a similar feature.

### Science & Futuristic Technology

[### The SpaceX IPO and Data Centers in Space (22 minute read)](https://stratechery.com/2026/the-spacex-ipo-and-data-centers-in-space/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

SpaceX's arguments for its $2 trillion valuation make sense assuming a huge number of engineering challenges are solved. Starship will need to work, SpaceX will need to be able to get a sufficient supply of the right kinds of chips, compute demand needs to grow massively larger, and agentic inference needs to unbundle current architectures. The risk attached to any of these assumptions should be discounted from any valuation put on the business. Elon Musk has already pushed humanity forward on multiple vectors and SpaceX has already invented a lot, so it's not totally implausible that the company may reach its goals, but there's still real risk.

[### US Space Force confirms SpaceX will build sensor-to-shooter targeting network (6 minute read)](https://arstechnica.com/space/2026/05/us-space-force-confirms-spacex-will-build-sensor-to-shooter-targeting-network/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

SpaceX has won a contract to provide the US military with a means of distributing space-based sensing and targeting data. The $2.29 billion firm-fixed-price agreement will result in the creation of a network based on technology originally developed for SpaceX's Starlink global Internet constellation. It will act as the core communications layer for US Space Force war-fighting systems, ensuring sensors and shooters are connected continuously, globally, and securely. SpaceX is required to deliver a full operational prototype by the end of 2027.
