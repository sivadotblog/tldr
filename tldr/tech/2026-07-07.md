### Big Tech & Startups

[### Nvidia's next-gen AI rack system delayed to 2028 on manufacturing snags (4 minute read)](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/06/nvidia-kyber-rack-system-delays-manufacturing-taiwan-rubin-chips-.html?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Nvidia's Kyber rack-scale architecture has been delayed by more than 12 months to 2028. The server cabinet is designed to house 144 of the company's 2027 Rubin Ultra chips so they can work together as one giant computer. The delay is due to difficulties in manufacturing a key circuit board at the heart of the system. It is a sign that Nvidia's breakneck annual release cadence is colliding with manufacturing limits.

[### Apple's iPhone Ultra will be double the price of the iPhone 17 Pro Max (2 minute read)](https://mashable.com/tech/apple-iphone-ultra-price-rumor?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

The iPhone Ultra will be announced in September together with the iPhone 18 Pro and Pro Max. It will start shipping about a month later. The foldable phone is estimated to be priced at around $2,400. It is likely that the phone will sell out immediately after pre-orders open, and then there will be delays running through December.

### Science & Futuristic Technology

[### Egypt Is Building a New Nile (20 minute read)](https://www.theb1m.com/video/egypt-is-building-a-new-nile?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Egypt is attempting to create a new agricultural landscape to relieve mounting pressure on the Nile Delta. The New Delta product is a plan that involves recycling water, moving it across the desert, and bringing it to new land for cultivation. Early satellite images show rapid progress, but questions remain about how sustainable the transformation will be. Much of the water currently used to irrigate the new fields is coming from underground aquifers, which are not easily replenished.

[### The Robots Are Here (32 minute read)](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/the-robots-are-here?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Robots decouple capital from human labor. This means production capacity becomes tied to how many robots one can make or afford. Robust robot capacity enables things that were previously impossible. This post features an interview with robotics experts about why robots are the real general-purpose technology, how everyone keeps underestimating Unitree, China's edge, why you can't AI your way out of a hardware or supply chain problem, and what the US should do next.
