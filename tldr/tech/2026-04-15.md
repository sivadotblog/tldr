### Science & Futuristic Technology

[### Max Hodak's Science Corp. is preparing to place its first sensor in a human brain (5 minute read)](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/14/max-hodaks-science-corp-is-preparing-to-place-its-first-sensor-in-a-human-brain/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Science Corporation plans to start human trials for its biohybrid brain-computer interface. It has hired a top neurobiologist to surgically place a sensor for a future interface into a patient's brain. The sensor will be implanted into the skull, but rest on top of the brain. The final device will be embedded with lab-grown neurons that can be stimulated with pulses of light and are designed to naturally integrate with the neurons in a patient's brain.

[### Apple chooses Amazon satellites for iPhone, years after rejecting Starlink offer (6 minute read)](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/04/amazon-to-merge-with-globalstar-become-iphones-primary-satellite-provider/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Amazon has entered into an agreement with Apple to provide satellite service for its devices using the Amazon Leo network. Amazon recently signed a merger agreement to buy satellite operator Globalstar, which had already partnered with Apple for satellite messaging on the iPhone. Buying Globalstar will help Amazon enter the Direct-to-Device market, in which satellites provide connectivity to mobile phones. Apple is working on a framework for third-party apps to let developers add satellite connections to their apps.

[### Inside GitHub's Fake Star Economy (16 minute read)](https://awesomeagents.ai/news/github-fake-stars-investigation/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

A study found 6 million fake stars across 18,617 repositories. AI/LLM repositories were the largest non-malicious category. There are at least a dozen websites selling stars for between $0.03 to $0.85 each. The FTC introduced a rule in 2024 banning fake social influence metrics with a penalty of $53,088 per violation. The SEC has already charged startup founders for inflating traction metrics during fundraising.

[### Google introduces “Skills” in Chrome to make Gemini prompts instantly reusable (2 minute read)](https://arstechnica.com/google/2026/04/google-introduces-skills-in-chrome-to-make-gemini-prompts-instantly-reusable/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Chrome has implemented 'Skills' in Chrome - reusable prompts that can be accessed while browsing with a single click. Skills make it easier to repeat tasks that were already possible with Gemini in Chrome. The feature makes it easy to save favorite prompts, making them easy to quickly access. The desktop version of Chrome will remember saved Skills across devices.
