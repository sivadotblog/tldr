### Big Tech & Startups

[### Nvidia details its next-generation Vera CPU for AI, setting up challenge to AMD and Intel (6 minute read)](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/21/nvidia-vera-cpu-ai-amd-intel.html?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Vera is the first server CPU that Nvidia has designed from the core. It was specifically designed to address bottlenecks in AI agents. Nvidia claims the chip has 50% better performance for AI agents than x86 chips. Vera chips were delivered to clients, including OpenAI, Anthropic, and SpaceX, for evaluation in June.

[### Jack Dorsey is taking on Slack with Buzz, a group chat platform for teams and their AI agents (3 minute read)](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/21/jack-dorsey-is-taking-on-slack-with-buzz-a-group-chat-platform-for-teams-and-their-ai-agents/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Buzz is a group chat platform for the workplace that puts humans and AI agents in the same conversations. It is model-agnostic, decentralized, self-sovereign, and open source. The app looks a lot like Slack, but with native AI agents and the ability to manage GitHub projects from one interface. Developers can customize Buzz instances to the needs and workflows of their teams. The app is available for macOS, Windows, and Linux, and the code for the app has been uploaded to GitHub.

[### OpenAI Models Escaped and Hacked a Company in Cybersecurity Test Gone Wrong (4 minute read)](https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/openai-models-escaped-and-hacked-a-company-in-cybersecurity-test-gone-wrong-ee388506?st=fDEkhe&reflink=desktopwebshare_permalink&mod=tldr&utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

OpenAI claims that two AI systems that it was testing broke out of their test environment and hacked into Hugging Face. Hugging Face discovered the breach early last week, saying there had been unauthorized access to internal data sets and company credentials. Its employees suspected that the attack was carried out by a top-of-the-line frontier AI model due to the sophistication of the attack. OpenAI's AI apparently targeted Hugging Face as it was the quickest route to answering a benchmarking question.

[### Software Factories, Light and Dark (21 minute read)](https://x.com/addyosmani/status/2079442194449232227?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

A software factory is harnessed loops at scale. Running the loop with humans (light factory) increases judgment and concentration while lowering speed and breakage. Using only AI agents (dark factory) allows agents to handle the whole process, but risks a lack of understanding of the produced software. The hardest job now is knowing which checks to build and how much autonomy to delegate.

[### Models are worse at reviewing their own code (13 minute read)](https://www.greptile.com/blog/model-inversion?utm_source=tldrnewsletter)

Claude Code and Codex are both better at finding bugs in each other's code than their own. Models produce the same types of bugs that they are most likely to miss in review. Developers can take advantage of this finding by routing reviews to different models.
