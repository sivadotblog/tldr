### Deep Dives & Analysis

[### Kimi K3's Design Secret may be in its Thinking Traces (6 minute read)](https://notes.designarena.ai/kimi-k3s-design-secret-may-be-in-its-thinking-traces/?utm_source=tldrai)

Kimi K3 uses an extreme amount of thinking tokens. It uses over 12 times more reasoning than Claude Opus 4.8 and over double that of Kimi K2.6. The model's performance is primarily due to its unique chain-of-thought approach in which it iterates upon designs like a full AI agent would, but inside its chain of thought. This article looks at Kimi K3's thinking traces to explain the model's performance.

[### How to Build a Frontier Model Factory (48 minute read)](https://www.latent.space/p/poolside?utm_source=tldrai)

Poolside co-CEO Eiso Kant explains how a compact research team built the training infrastructure behind Laguna S, an 118B mixture-of-experts model reported to outperform much larger open-weight systems.

[### A GPU-Hour Isn't a Commodity If You Need Four of Them (6 minute read)](https://davefriedman.substack.com/p/a-gpu-hour-isnt-a-commodity-if-you?utm_source=tldrai)

Compute pricing coverage runs on headline rates, but many workloads don't consume them that way. Training, fine-tuning, and high-throughput inference need multiple identical GPUs in the same machine or cluster. A company that buys H100 futures to hedge a future four- or eight-GPU requirement carries real basis risk. The futures will hedge a rise in the broad H100 price and do nothing about the lack of co-located capacity.
