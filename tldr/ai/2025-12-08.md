### Engineering & Research

[### Announcing Rnj-1: Building Instruments of Intelligence (14 minute read)](https://essential.ai/research/rnj-1?utm_source=tldrai)

Rnj-1 is a world-class pair of base and instruction-tuned large language models. The 8B model roughly follows the open-source Gemma 3 architecture. The Rnj-1 Base and Instruct models compare favorably against similarly sized open weight models. This post summarizes the key capabilities of the models and covers the background behind their development.

[### Titans + MIRAS: Helping AI have long-term memory (12 minute read)](https://research.google/blog/titans-miras-helping-ai-have-long-term-memory/?utm_source=tldrai)

Google Research has released two new papers introducing an architecture (Titans) and a theoretical blueprint (MIRAS) that combine the speed of recurrent neural networks with the accuracy of transformers. Together, Titans and MIRAS advance the concept of test-time memorization, the ability of an AI model to maintain long-term memory. They introduce a meaningful shift toward real-time adaptation. The architecture actively learns and updates its own parameters as data streams in, enabling the model to incorporate new, specific details into its core knowledge instantly.

[### cuTile Python (GitHub Repo)](https://github.com/NVIDIA/cutile-python?utm_source=tldrai)

cuTile Python is a programming language for writing parallel kernels for NVIDIA GPUs. It automatically leverages advanced hardware capabilities while providing portability across different NVIDIA GPU architectures. cuTile enables the latest hardware features without requiring code changes.

[### Computer-science Reinforcement Learning Got Rewards Wrong (6 minute read)](https://gist.github.com/yoavg/3eb3e722d38e887a0a8ac151c62d9617?utm_source=tldrai)

In reinforcement learning, the reward is part of the agent, not part of the environment. Once we start thinking in terms of the reward translation as being part of the agent, we can also start thinking of ways in which the agent can affect the reward translation mechanism. It could be dynamic and change across time, be tied to policy, or even learned. The shift in perspective creates many possible options, just by changing the notation - because notation matters.
