[### $350B (1 minute read)](https://sherwood.news/tech/google-in-funding-talks-with-anthropic-at-a-potential-usd350-billion/?utm_source=tldrai)

Google plans to invest even more money into Anthropic. It is now in talks for a new funding round that could value Anthropic at $350 billion. The new deal could involve Google providing additional cloud computing services to Anthropic, a convertible note, or a priced funding round early next year. Google last month announced that it had inked a deal worth tens of billions for Anthropic to access Google's AI compute.

### Deep Dives & Analysis

[### 5 Thoughts on Kimi K2 Thinking (8 minute read)](https://www.interconnects.ai/p/kimi-k2-thinking-what-it-means?utm_source=tldrai)

Kimi K2 Thinking, a reasoning MoE model developed by China's Moonshot AI team, shows just how many people are learning quickly to train excellent AI models. Early reports note a distinctive style and writing quality - the model is beating leading closed models on some benchmarks such as Humanity's Last Exam and BrowseComp. It is the closest open models have been to the closed frontier of performance ever.

[### Forecasts of AI & Economic Growth (12 minute read)](https://tecunningham.github.io/posts/2025-10-19-forecasts-of-AI-growth.html?utm_source=tldrai)

Most economists forecast 0.1-1.5% annual excess growth through 2035, while AI insiders predict 3-30%. Most economists, though, assume that today's ChatGPT is essentially the ceiling of AI progress and that visions of a virtual AI coworker won't really materialize, but how is it possible that cheap, PhD-level intelligence wouldn't boost productivity? The forecasts imply that either AGI will be far less general than claimed or that adoption will be dramatically slower than historical technology diffusion. AI's 2024 economic footprint appears to be around 0.5% of GDP.

[### Accumulating Context Changes The Beliefs of Language Models (32 minute read)](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.01805?utm_source=tldrai)

Extended conversations can reshape how AI models answer the same question. After 10 rounds of debate about moral dilemmas, GPT-5 reversed its position 54.7% of the time when asked again. When models changed their stated beliefs, they also chose different tools to accomplish tasks.
