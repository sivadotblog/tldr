[### Kubernetes v1.35: Timbernetes (The World Tree Release) (25 minute read)](https://kubernetes.io/blog/2025/12/17/kubernetes-v1-35-release/?utm_source=tldrdevops)

Kubernetes v1.35 features 60 enhancements, including 17 stable, 19 beta, and 22 alpha features, most notably graduating in-place updates for Pod resources and native workload identity with automated certificate rotation to general availability. The update also removed cgroup v1 support, deprecated ipvs mode in kube-proxy, and announced the retirement of the Ingress NGINX controller.

### Opinions & Tutorials

[### Let's talk about GitHub Actions (6 minute read)](https://github.blog/news-insights/product-news/lets-talk-about-github-actions/?utm_source=tldrdevops)

GitHub rebuilt the core architecture of Actions to scale reliably to 71 million jobs per day, then shipped long-requested improvements, including YAML anchors, deeper reusable workflows, larger caches, and more inputs. Further UX, scheduling, and parallelism enhancements have been planned for 2026.

[### Your job is to deliver code you have proven to work (4 minute read)](https://simonwillison.net/2025/Dec/18/code-proven-to-work/?utm_source=tldrdevops)

Engineers are responsible for delivering code that is proven to work, not dumping large, untested AI-generated changes onto reviewers and shifting the burden of validation onto others. Proof requires both manual testing and automated tests, and even when using coding agents, the human remains accountable for ensuring evidence of correctness accompanies every PR.

Pulumi is the simplest and most broadly compatible migration path. It offers similar multi-language support and built-in conversion tools for CDKTF projects.

[### Inside the feature store powering real-time AI in Dropbox Dash (7 minute read)](https://dropbox.tech/machine-learning/feature-store-powering-realtime-ai-in-dropbox-dash?utm_source=tldrdevops)

Dropbox developed a custom feature store to power the AI-driven search in its Dash product, enabling rapid retrieval of relevant files by understanding complex user queries across company content. This hybrid architecture leveraged Feast for orchestration, Spark for computation, and Dropbox's in-house Dynovault for low-latency lookups, alongside a custom Go service that achieved sub-100ms p95 latencies for thousands of feature lookups per query.
